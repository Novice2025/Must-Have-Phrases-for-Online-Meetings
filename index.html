ONLINE MEETING PROTOCOL

1. Start & Agenda 2. Screen Share 3. Clarity & Interrupt 4. Close & Action

Business English | Remote Work

Must-Have Phrases for Online Meetings

Simple, clear expressions for welcoming, clarifying, interrupting, and summarizing, ensuring you sound professional and are easily understood.

1. Starting the Meeting & Setting the Agenda

Welcome, Purpose, and Duration

These phrases clearly state the objective and time commitment, managing participant expectations.

  • Welcome: **Welcome everyone**, today's meeting is about... (The purpose of today's meeting is to...)
  • Agenda: I sent you the agenda before the meeting, so you should all have a copy.
  • Duration: The meeting is going to last **no more than one hour**, so everybody will be out of here by three o'clock.
  • Ready to Start: Okay, **are we good to go?** / Okay, **let's get started, shall we?**
  • Informal Start: Okay, **let's kick off the meeting with...** / Let's **get the ball rolling** with a brief rundown of the last meeting.

2. Screen Sharing Protocol

Announcing and Confirming Shared Content

Always vocalize when you are sharing your screen or documents so participants know what to expect.

  • Permission/Action: Can I **share my screen** with you? / I'm going to **share my screen** with you now.
  • Presentation: I'm going to **put up my presentation** now (or share the powerpoint presentation).
  • Confirmation: **Can you see my screen clearly?** / Can you see what I'm showing you?
  • Feedback: If you've got any comments, you can put them in the **chat line**.

3. Clarity, Interruptions, and Technical Issues

Requesting Clarity and Repetition

  • Pace: **Could you speak more slowly, please?**
  • Volume: **Could you speak up, please?** I didn't quite hear it.
  • Detail: **Could you explain that again?** / Could you repeat the last bit?
  • Missed Point: I didn't quite **get that**. Perhaps you could go through that again.
  • Visual Error: My picture froze for a minute, I just missed the last bit you said. **Could you repeat it, please?**

Polite Interruption and Technical Troubleshooting

  • Formal Interrupt: **Could I interrupt you for a minute?** / Can I stop you there quickly?
  • Informal Interrupt: Would you mind if I **jumped in here**? (or **butted in** here?)
  • Clarification: **Just to clarify**, what you're saying is...?
  • Hold Request: **Hold on a minute, please.** We don't seem to have discussed that in much detail.
  • Audio/Video Issues: **I'm having problems hearing you.** / Your **picture's frozen**. / There seems to be an **echo**.

4. Transitions, Summary, and Closing

Action Points and Follow-up

  • Transition: Okay, let's **move on to the next point**. / **Moving on swiftly**, let's talk about this.
  • Follow-up Meeting: Let's **pencil in another meeting** for next Wednesday to pick up on these points.
  • Summary Intro: **In summary**, we're going to do this. / Let me **summarize** by saying this.
  • Ending the Call: I think we can **leave it here for today**. / I think this is a **good place to stop** or to end.
  • Next Steps: I'll send you an email and we will start again same time and go through the **remaining points**.

Key Jargon Recap

These three terms are essential metaphors for driving meeting momentum:

Kick Off: To formally start the meeting.

Get the Ball Rolling: To initiate the discussion/start moving forward.

Pencil In: To tentatively schedule a follow-up meeting.
